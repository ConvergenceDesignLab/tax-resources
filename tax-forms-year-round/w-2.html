Long after your summer job is done, around late January, you will receive a W-2 from your old employer. This form is used to file your taxes.

If you wrote ‘exempt’ on your w4 you probably do not need to file a tax return. However, if you are getting some money withheld on your paycheck, you might want to consider filing a tax return to get that money back in a refund. Go to https://irs.treasury.gov/freetaxprep Be sure to keep all your tax paperwork in a safe place.

If you want help with your taxes but cannot make it to a tax preparation service, think about filing your taxes online with IRS Free File. Remember, when filing your taxes, you are able to deduct some expenses from your taxable income, which reduces your tax bill - usually through a standard deduction. Don’t forget about this when filing your taxes.

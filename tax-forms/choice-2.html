Independent Contractors vs Employment

  • When working, some positions such as Uber, Lyft or some summer youth employment jobs are considered independent contractors, while similar positions such as city bus drivers are considered employees.
  • There are many rules that help decide whether someone is a contractor or employee, but you can recognize how you are being classified by the forms you are asked to fill out on or before your first day.
  • If you are an employee, an employer will ask you to fill out a form W-4, which contains questions that determine your withholding. Withholding is the money that is withheld from your paycheck each pay period.
  • If you are being classified as an independent contractor, you may be asked to complete form W-9 when you start working, and taxes will generally NOT be withheld from your paycheck. It is important to set aside money each month as you may have to pay taxes on the money that you have earned later.

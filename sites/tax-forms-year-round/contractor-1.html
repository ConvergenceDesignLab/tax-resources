Tax information for Independent Contractors

A W-9 is a form that is used to collect basic information from independent contractors like their name and social security number. The W-9 will be used by the organization that you are working with as an independent contractor to generate a 1099 form at the end of the year, if you received more than $600. Click here to view a W-9 form.

To complete your W-9, read all of the information carefully and complete the form to reflect your specific situation, but for MOST youth:

  1. Write your full legal name on line 1.
  2. Skip the business name on line 2 (unless this pertains to you).
  3. Select that you are an individual for line 3 (unless another selection applies).
  4. Skip line item 4 (unless this applies).
  5. Complete your correct address for lines 5 & 6.
  6. Skip line 7 (unless this applies).
  7. Carefully and legibly enter your social security number for Part I. (Almost all youth will enter a social security number and NOT an employer identification number. Skip the employer identification number unless it applies.)
  8. Review all certifications, and if they are all correct, sign and date the form.
  9. Your W-9 includes identifying information such as your social security number, so keep this form safe and promptly turn it in to your job site organization.
